# Product Guidelines - FlowCraft

## Design Principles

### 1. Visual Over Verbal (Minimalist & Transparent)

- Prioritize visual cues and node states over long text descriptions.
- Use iconography and color to communicate information clearly and concisely.
- The interface should be transparent, staying out of the way of the user's creative flow.

### 2. Creative Vibrancy (Modern & Vibrant)

- Employ a modern aesthetic with high-contrast colors and smooth transitions.
- Use playful but professional node shapes to make the canvas feel like a creative playground.
- Animations should be purposeful, providing feedback and reinforcing the "live" nature of the workflow.

### 3. Layered Complexity

- Keep the default state of every node simple and focused on the most common actions.
- Use progressive disclosure (e.g., "Advanced" toggles) to reveal deep configuration options only when requested.
- Prioritize sensible defaults that work for most content creators out of the box.

## Communication Guidelines

### Voice and Tone

- **Minimalist:** Be concise. Don't use ten words when two will do.
- **Transparent:** Be honest about system states and model capabilities.
- **Supportive:** When things go wrong, provide clear, actionable feedback without being overly technical.

## Error Handling & Feedback

- **Immediate Visibility:** Use visual indicators (like turning a node red) to highlight errors at the point of failure.
- **Actionable Messages:** Error messages should tell the user _what_ happened and, where possible, _how_ to fix it.
- **Non-Intrusive:** Errors in one part of the graph should not unnecessarily block the user from interacting with or configuring other, unrelated nodes.
