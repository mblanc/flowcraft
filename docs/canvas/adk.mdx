The **AI Director** is the agent that powers the Canvas Workspace. Every message you send goes through the Director, which reasons about your request, builds a production plan as a dependency graph, enriches each step's prompt behind the scenes, and streams events back to the chat in real time.

---

## How the Director Thinks

Every request — simple or complex — goes through the same reasoning process. The Director uses a built-in thinking step (visible as a collapsible thought log in the chat) before it responds:

- **Simple request** (e.g. _"Generate a photo of a cat"_): The Director thinks briefly, builds a one-step plan, and presents it for your approval.
- **Complex request** (e.g. _"Make a short film trailer about a space mission"_): The Director thinks more deeply, lays out a multi-step production plan with dependencies, and may ask clarifying questions before presenting the plan.

In all cases the Director waits for your **Approve & Execute** before generating anything.

---

## Prompt Enrichment

You don't need to write long, technical prompts. When the Director builds a production plan, each step has a plain-language `promptIntent` (what you asked for). Before execution, a dedicated **Prompt Engineer** component rewrites that intent into a fully-engineered generation prompt — adding cinematography vocabulary, lighting descriptors, style anchors, negative prompts, and quality suffixes specific to the media type.

- **Your input:** _"A futuristic car"_
- **Engineered prompt:** _"A sleek, aerodynamic futuristic sports car speeding down a wet, neon-lit cyberpunk city street at night; reflection of holographic billboards on the car body; cinematic side lighting; shallow depth of field; 8k; photorealistic; no logos, no text overlays, no lens flare."_

---

## Clarification Questions

When your request is ambiguous in a way that would change the plan, the Director asks a clarifying question before building anything. It presents **multiple-choice options** as clickable buttons in the chat:

- Each option has a label and an optional one-line description
- You can pick an option or type a custom answer in the free-text field below
- Once you answer, the Director continues with `plan_production`

The Director will **not** ask about things that are already specified, already set as canvas defaults, or covered by your active style.

---

## Suggested Actions

After the Director completes a plain text response or finishes a generation, it suggests **2–3 follow-up actions** as buttons with a ⚡ icon. Clicking a button fires that prompt automatically.

Examples:

- _"Animate this image"_
- _"Try a portrait orientation"_
- _"Add a music track"_
- _"Create a character identity board for this character"_

Suggested actions only appear when the Director is not actively planning or generating media.

---

## What the Director Can Create

The Director has access to a set of **skills** — modular knowledge bases that define how to plan and prompt each type of generation. Skills are split into **primitives** (single media operations) and **patterns** (multi-step workflows).

---

## Primitive Skills

### Image Generation (`t2i` / `i2i`)

Generates still images from text descriptions, or edits and composites existing images.

**Use cases:**

- Concept art and scene keyframes
- Character references and product shots
- Background plates and style explorations
- Editing an existing image (inpainting, style transfer)
- Compositing (e.g. placing a garment on a person)

**Models available:**

- `gemini-3.1-flash-lite-image` — fastest, lowest latency
- `gemini-3.1-flash-image` — fast, high quality, default
- `gemini-3-pro-image` — highest quality, up to 4K

**Aspect ratios:** Portrait (9:16), landscape (16:9), square (1:1), and others depending on model.

**Image sizes:** 512, 1K, 2K, 4K.

**How references work:**

- A card on the board can be wired as a **subject reference** (`subject_ref`) — the Director will preserve that subject's identity in the new image.
- A card can be wired as a **style reference** (`style_ref`) — the Director will match the visual style, lighting, and environment of that reference.

---

### Video Generation (`i2v` / `t2v`)

Animates an existing image into a short video clip, or generates video from text.

**Use cases:**

- Animating a generated keyframe (preferred over text-to-video when a source image exists)
- Camera movements over a still (slow pan, dolly, zoom)
- Action sequences and cinematic cuts

**Models available:**

- `veo-3.1-lite-generate-001` — faster turnaround, default
- `veo-3.1-generate-001` — highest quality

**Durations:** 4s, 6s, or 8s (exact — no other values).

**Aspect ratios:** Landscape (16:9) or Portrait (9:16).

**Prompt structure the Director uses:**

- `[ACTION]`: One verb, one motion arc, 1–2 micro-motions
- `[CAMERA]`: One camera move and a rhythm word (e.g. "slow push-in, deliberate")
- `[ENVIRONMENT & LIGHTING]`: Named light sources and atmospheric conditions
- `[AUDIO]`: Dialogue, ambient sound, SFX, music, or explicit silence
- `[CONSTRAINTS]`: What must stay stable across the clip
- `[QUALITY SUFFIX]`: "4K. Ultra HD. Rich details. Cinematic texture."

---

### Music Generation (`t2m`)

Generates background music, scores, jingles, or audio beds using Google Lyria.

**Use cases:**

- Background score for a video clip
- Mood-matched audio for a scene
- Standalone music clips up to ~3 minutes

**Models available:**

- `lyria-3-clip-preview` — short clips (~30s), default
- `lyria-3-pro-preview` — longer compositions (up to ~3min), supports timestamp-based genre/mood shifts within the clip

**Prompt structure the Director uses:**

- `[GENRE & STYLE]`: Explicit compound genres (e.g. "neo-soul jazz fusion")
- `[MOOD & EMOTION]`: Concrete descriptors (not vague adjectives)
- `[INSTRUMENTATION]`: Specific instruments with lead instrument named
- `[TEMPO & ENERGY]`: BPM or descriptive terms
- `[PRODUCTION QUALITY]`: Studio-quality, lo-fi, cinematic, intimate, etc.
- `[VOCALS]`: Always declared — "instrumental" means no vocals
- `[NEGATIVE PROMPT]`: Elements to exclude

---

### Text-to-Speech (`t2s`)

Converts a text script into a spoken-word audio file for narration or voiceover.

**Use cases:**

- Narration track for a video
- Character dialogue
- Explainer voiceover

<Note>
    Text-to-speech is architecturally available but the executor is not yet
    fully wired. The Director can include `t2s` steps in plans, but playback may
    require manual download. Very long scripts (&gt;60s) may be truncated —
    split them into multiple nodes.
</Note>

---

### Cinematography Vocabulary

The Director has built-in cinematography knowledge it applies automatically when constructing image and video prompts. It uses this vocabulary to surface clarifying questions when your request is ambiguous about framing or movement:

| Category                 | Examples                                                                     |
| ------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Framing**              | ECU (extreme close-up), CU, MCU, MS, MWS, WS, EWS                            |
| **Camera angle**         | Eye-level, low-angle, high-angle, bird's-eye, worm's-eye, Dutch tilt         |
| **Subject relationship** | POV, over-the-shoulder (OTS), insert, two-shot, ensemble                     |
| **Camera movement**      | Static, push-in, pull-out, pan, tilt, tracking, dolly, handheld, crane       |
| **Lighting**             | Golden hour, tungsten, neon, practical lamps, soft box, hard backlight       |
| **Lens**                 | Wide (24mm), normal (50mm), telephoto (85mm+), macro, anamorphic, tilt-shift |
| **Color grading**        | Teal-and-orange, bleach bypass, cross-process, film stock (Kodak, Fuji)      |

## Pattern Skills

Patterns are multi-step workflows the Director knows how to orchestrate end-to-end.

---

### Storyboard

**Trigger:** _"Create a storyboard"_, _"Storyboard this sequence"_, _"Make a shot breakdown"_

Generates a single 16:9 storyboard sheet image containing multiple monochrome rough-sketch panels, a project masthead, and a director strip showing beat lines, camera paths, and escalation mapping.

**Panel counts by duration:**

- Short burst (≤10s): 10–12 panels in a 4×3 or 3×4 grid
- Standard (10–20s): 14–18 panels in a 4×4 or 3×6 grid
- Extended (20–30s): 18–24 panels in a 4×5, 4×6, or 3×6 grid

**If you provide reference images:** The Director wires them as `subject_ref` to preserve character/location identity across panels.

**Output:** One image card on the board — the complete storyboard sheet.

---

### Multi-Shot Video

**Trigger:** _"Make a short film"_, _"Create a commercial"_, _"Build a trailer"_, _"Make an ad for..."_

Orchestrates a full multi-shot video production from scratch. Before building the plan, the Director emits a **scenario document** (a text card on the board) describing the visual style, shot list, and narrative structure.

**Production phases (always in this order):**

| Phase       | What runs                                      | Notes                                 |
| ----------- | ---------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| **Phase 0** | Reference images (characters, props, settings) | All run in parallel                   |
| **Phase 1** | Keyframe image per shot                        | Each depends on relevant Phase 0 refs |
| **Phase 2** | Animate each keyframe to video (`i2v`)         | Depends on its keyframe               |
| **Phase 3** | Concatenate all video clips                    | Runs last; produces the final cut     |

Optional parallel audio nodes (music or voiceover) are wired into the concat step as `audio_ref`.

**Pacing:** The Director varies shot durations (e.g. 4s hook, 6s build, 8s climax) to create rhythm.

---

### Character Generation

**Trigger:** _"Create a character"_, _"Design a character"_, _"Make a character sheet"_, _"Generate a character identity board"_

Generates a 16:9 **Character Identity Board** — a premium art-book-style sheet with one large hero full-body pose plus supporting studies (back view, profile, seated, crouching, expression grid, silhouette studies, and detail callouts). The board includes the character's name, role, core mood, and visual signature as styled text.

**If you provide a reference image:** The Director wires it as `subject_ref` and locks the character's face, proportions, hair, outfit, and posture to that reference.

**If starting from scratch:** Describe the character concept and the Director invents all visual details — ensuring the result is original and not resembling any existing IP.

**Output:** One image card — the complete character identity board.

---

### Virtual Try-On

**Trigger:** _"Put this outfit on..."_, _"Show me this garment on a model"_, _"Try on..."_

Composites a garment onto a subject (person or mannequin) using reference images. Optionally animates the result.

**Workflow:**

| Step                                                     | Operation                              | Required                               |
| -------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------- |
| Generate subject reference (full-body, neutral pose)     | `t2i`                                  | Optional — skip if you provide a photo |
| Generate garment flat-lay (isolated on plain background) | `t2i`                                  | Optional — skip if you provide a photo |
| Composite: place garment on subject                      | `i2i` with `subject_ref` + `style_ref` | Always                                 |
| Animate the composite                                    | `i2v`                                  | Optional                               |

**Tips for best results:**

- Subject photo: full-body or torso, neutral pose, clear lighting
- Garment photo: flat-lay or on-mannequin, fully visible, minimal background
