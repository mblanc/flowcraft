Lumino — Trailer Architecture
Target Duration: 27 seconds exact
Format: Cinematic 16:9 | Painterly-realistic matte painting style
Tone: Nocturnal tone poem — intimate, atmospheric, quietly devastating

VISUAL STYLE ANCHOR
Deep navy and charcoal city nights. Wet cobblestones bleeding electric blue and amber neon. Mist and fog rolling through alleys. Warm cream and pale gold light in domestic spaces. Palette shifts from cold (survival) to warm (home) across the trailer's arc. NOT cartoon or cel-shaded — painterly-photorealist, cinematic matte painting throughout. All keyframes reference the locked visual direction established in the mood frames.

AUDIO DIRECTION
Score Arc:
Solo cello, bare and low — a single sustained note under the cold open, barely audible, as if the city is breathing. Sparse piano notes enter during world-building, each one deliberate, leaving silence between them. Strings begin a slow swell through tension escalation, building in layers — violin enters, intensity mounts. At crescendo, the score swells to full emotional peak — strings and piano together, warm and aching. Hard cut to silence on blackout. Title card: three beats of absolute quiet. Stinger plays with a single unresolved piano note, hanging.
Voiceover (VO):
Two lines, whispered by Ellie — soft, intimate, unhurried. Delivered not as narration but as memory:
"He'd been watching the windows for so long."
(Beat.)
"He just needed someone to leave the door open."
VO Line 1 begins mid-world-building and carries through tension escalation. VO Line 2 lands at or just before the crescendo beat.
Sound Design:
Cold open: faint rain ambience, distant city hum, near silence
World building: rain on cobblestones, neon buzz, a distant car
Tension escalation: wind cutting, cold ambient drone, silence between shots
Crescendo: score overwhelms SFX entirely — pure emotion
Blackout: total silence
Stinger: one environmental sound — a soft rain tap, or the purr resonance of a cat

Camera: Extremely slow push forward — almost imperceptible drift toward the cat. The city breathes.
Duration: 2.5s
Pacing note: Slow. Atmospheric. Let the rain register.
Text overlay: None

Shot 03 — "Watching the Windows"
Purpose: Establish Lumino's longing. The warmth exists — he's outside it.
What viewer sees: Low-angle shot looking up a stone wall at warm amber windows glowing from an apartment above. Lumino sits on a fire escape landing below, silhouetted, looking up. Raindrops catch the amber light. The gap between him and those windows is everything.
Camera: Low angle, locked. Rain falls between lens and subject.
Duration: 2s
Pacing note: Still. Contemplative. VO Line 1 begins here — "He'd been watching the windows for so long."

Duration: 6 seconds | Cumulative: 0:07–0:13

Shot 04 — "The Garden's Last Light"
Purpose: Brief breath of warmth before the loss sequence — the life he had.
What viewer sees: The hidden secret garden — overgrown ivy, crumbling stone walls, golden-hour amber light. Two or three cats lounging in pools of warm dappled sunlight. Peaceful. But the light is late-day, fading. A sense of borrowed time.
Camera: Slow gentle pan left to right across the garden. Golden warmth.
Duration: 1.5s
Pacing note: Transitional — warm but fleeting. Doesn't linger.
Text overlay: None
Keyframe approach: PAIRED — left edge of garden to right edge pan
Shot 05 — "Winter Moves In"
Purpose: Turn. The loss begins. Pacing quickens.
What viewer sees: The same alley as Shot 02, but now blanketed in grey-white snow. Sparse. Cold. Empty. A torn piece of cardboard — Lumino's old shelter — left behind, half-buried. No cat in frame. The absence is the subject.
Camera: Slow descending tilt — begin on grey winter sky, settle on the empty cardboard.
Duration: 1.5s
Pacing note: Cut is abrupt from Shot 04 — the warmth is gone. Score begins building.
Text overlay: None
Keyframe approach: PAIRED — sky down to ground tilt
Shot 06 — "Crossing the Bridge"
Purpose: Lumino alone at his lowest. Maximum isolation.
What viewer sees: A fog-shrouded pedestrian bridge over dark water, seen from a distance. A single small figure — Lumino — walks slowly into the mist, becoming a silhouette, becoming a suggestion, becoming nothing. The fog closes around him.
Camera: Locked wide. The bridge recedes into white mist. Scale is devastating.
Duration: 1.5s
Pacing note: Held just long enough to feel the weight. VO Line 2 begins here — "He just needed someone to leave the door open."
Text overlay: None
Keyframe approach: ANIMATED STILL — fog animated, Lumino walks slowly into white
Shot 07 — "The Bench"
Purpose: The lowest point — and the threshold of change.
What viewer sees: Snow-covered park bench. Lumino curled beneath it on the frozen ground, barely visible in low light. Snow falls gently. Utterly still and quiet. But in the far corner of the frame — a pair of small child's boots approach slowly, tentatively.
Camera: Locked wide, slightly low to the ground. Boots enter from edge of frame.
Duration: 1.5s
Pacing note: Score reaches its quiet devastation peak here before turning. Tension peaks.
Text overlay: None
Keyframe approach: PAIRED — empty bench shot to boots-entering-frame shot

Shot 08 — "The Distance Between"
Purpose: The emotional fulcrum of the entire film. Connection across fear.
What viewer sees: Two-shot — Ellie crouched low on the snowy ground, mittened hand extended, still and patient. Lumino five inches away, tense but not running. Between their hands: a single small piece of bread on the white snow. Neither moving. The whole world held in that gap.
Camera: Ground-level, close. Shallow depth. Both faces slightly visible — her face soft with hope, his eyes amber and wary.
Duration: 1.5s
Pacing note: Score is at full swell. Time slows.
Text overlay: None
Keyframe approach: ANIMATED STILL — breath visible as vapor, snow falling
Shot 09 — "The Touch"
Purpose: Peak emotional release. The crossing of the threshold.
What viewer sees: Extreme close-up — Lumino's face pressing gently into the side of Ellie's mitten. His eyes close. Her mitten is warm red wool against the cold white world. Everything else falls away.
Camera: Macro close-up. Locked. Completely still.
Duration: 2s
Pacing note: The score peaks and holds — pure ache. Let this frame breathe.
Text overlay: None
Keyframe approach: ANIMATED STILL — tiny fur movement, breath vapor only
